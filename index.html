My name is Alankrit Tomar. I’m a 4th year PhD student at UT Austin currently working on Two-photon Fluorescence Microscopy for brain vasculature imaging applications.

My work includes

I design and build high-resolution microscopes to study the three-dimensional vascular and neuronal structures of the cortex at depths and resolutions pushing the limits of optical imaging.

Unlike conventional fluorescence microscopy, which requires a single photon to excite a fluorophore, multiphoton microscopy requires two (or three) photons with longer wavelengths to generate fluorescence. These longer excitation wavelengths allow multiphoton microscopy to achieve imaging depths significantly greater than confocal microscopy.

Vita

Non-Degenerate 2-Photon Imaging for Deep Cerebrovascular Imaging
Guide: Dr. Andrew Dunn, Department of Biomedical Engineering, UT Austin
Jan 2020- Present

Project entails investigating the microvasculature of the mouse cortex using 2-color, 2-photon microscopy. The 2-color system is being implemented by overlapping two tunable NIR-wavelength pulsed lasers spatially and temporally. Using this setup, the cerebrovascular structure can be imaged at depths, potentially greater than 1 mm. For the purpose of in vivo experiments, training in craniotomy surgical procedures was undertaken successfully. Responsible for mentoring of new students in craniotomy and imaging procedures.

Terahertz Communication using Optical Fibers
Guide: Dr. Ajeet Kumar, Delhi Technological University
August 2016-May 2019

Involved in design and analysis of simulation models of Photonic Crystal Fibers for application in short-range Terahertz Communication. Key goals included analysis of fiber designs that suffer from low material, confinement and bending losses. The simulation models were analyzed using COMSOL Multiphysics software.

Terbium-doped Glasses for Faraday Isolators
GGuide: Prof. Younès Messaddeq, Université Laval, Québec City
May 2018- August 2018

Involved in the development and analysis of Terbium-doped Fluorophosphate glasses for application in Faraday Isolators. Key responsibilities included choosing an appropriate base glass matrix for Terbium doping, fabrication of doped Fluorophosphate glasses and finally characterisation and analysis of fabricated glass samples and checking its feasibility as a Faraday Isolator.

Planar Plasmonic Terahertz Waveguides
Guide: Dr. Gagan Kumar Indian Institute of Technology, Guwahati
June 2017-July 2017

The project involved analyzing the interaction of Terahertz radiation with a periodically corrugated metal-dielectric waveguide. Simulation models of one-dimensional Terahertz waveguides were studied using the CST Microwave Studio software.

Development of the Personal Food Computer (PFC)
Department: Center for Innovation and Research, Havells India Ltd. Noida, India
December 2017-May 2019

Developed a machine (Personal Food Computer) that provided a highly controlled environment for growing crops hydroponically. The ultimate goal of the project was to grow crops that are extensively consumed in India. The project was undertaken under one of the biggest electrical appliances providers in India, Havells India Ltd.
